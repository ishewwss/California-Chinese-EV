Lower Prices, More Competition

One of the biggest reasons Chinese EVs could actually help California is because they’re way more affordable than most of the electric cars we have here. A lot of people want to switch to electric, but the prices are still crazy high, especially for students or families who are already dealing with rent, groceries, and everything else going up. Chinese companies like BYD and NIO figured out how to make EVs that are cheaper but still really advanced, which forces other car companies to step up their game. When more companies compete, prices drop, and regular people get better options.

California is already expensive enough, so having more affordable EV choices would make a huge difference. Instead of EVs being something only wealthier people can buy, Chinese EVs could help more people switch from gas cars without going broke. And honestly, competition is good — it pushes American companies to innovate faster and stop overcharging for features that should be standard by now.

Expanding the EV Market

Another thing people don’t always think about is how Chinese EVs could help grow the entire EV market instead of just taking customers away from American brands. If more people can afford EVs, then more charging stations get built, more jobs open up in EV infrastructure, and the whole clean‑energy system grows. California wants to ban new gas car sales by 2035, but that’s only possible if EVs become cheaper and easier to get.

So instead of seeing Chinese EVs as a threat, it might make more sense to see them as a boost. They could help California reach its clean‑energy goals faster while giving people more choices. And honestly, having more options is always better when you’re buying something as expensive as a car.
